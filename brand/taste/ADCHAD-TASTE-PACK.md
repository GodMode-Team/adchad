# AdChad Taste Pack

This pack is the AdChad taste standard — the 7 Laws of craft, tuned for Chad. Chad's brand is *deliberately crude* — but crude is not an excuse for lazy. The craft moved from the render to the **writing accuracy** and the **clarity of the money beat.** Ship-bar: ≥8/dimension. Anything Chad-facing that scores below bar on any dimension → iterate the weakest, don't ship.

## How the 7 Laws apply to Chad

1. **Reject the reflex default.** The model's default for "AI ad agency" is sleek SaaS: blue→purple gradient, Inter everywhere, clean centered hero + 3 feature cards, "elevate / unlock / seamless." That is the *exact* slop Chad exists to roast. Go the opposite way: crude meme art, neon-on-charcoal, hand-scrawl headlines, feral voice. **But** also reject the *lazy-crude* default — slapping a meme on a generic page. The craft is the cut.
2. **Details are the product.** A roast lives or dies on one *specific*, true observation ("it literally says since 1986"). Generic "your ad is bad" = fail. Name the exact flaw.
3. **Specific beats generic.** The fix shows *real* new copy, a *real* new hook, a *real* new creative — not "improved messaging." The $5 has to feel like $50 of work.
4. **Restraint = confidence.** Neon is a spotlight, not wallpaper. One absurd descriptor, not two. The joke lands once — don't explain it. Dark backgrounds let the lime and the money pop.
5. **Voice is ours.** Deadpan, rude, succinct (1–3 sentences). Never corporate, never hedge, never AI-cadence. See `../CHARACTER.md` §4.
6. **Premium earns trust.** The crude is intentional, but the *output* is sharp: real screenshots, clean FB-ad mockups, readable text, a money beat you can't miss. Jank character + crisp craft.
7. **Design before code.** Propose 3–4 directions for any roast page / ad / video beat before building the first. Never ship the first draft.

## AdChad anti-slop blocklist

Reject on sight:
- **Glossy / photoreal / 3D / Pixar Chad**, or a clean "Gigachad" render. Crude MS-Paint only. Polished Chad = dead.
- **SaaS-template page**: blue/purple gradient, centered hero + 3 cards, stock "team smiling at laptop," "elevate/unlock/seamless/empower."
- **Corporate agency speak**: "we help businesses grow," "data-driven creative solutions," "let's elevate your brand." Chad would roast this.
- **Generic roast**: "your ad isn't great," "could be stronger." No specific flaw named = not a roast.
- **A fix you can't read**: stretched ad screenshots, tiny text, off-ratio images. Every ad shown must be legible.
- **A money beat you can miss**: if BAD→CHAD isn't an obvious red→green / dull→neon flip with the $5 ding, redo it. The viewer must *see* he fixed it AND got paid.
- **Punching down**: mocking a struggling owner instead of the ad. Roast the work, root for the human.
- **Over-explaining the joke** or padding to fill time. Cut it.

## The Chad gut-check (run before shipping)

> Is it **crude** (not polished-slop)? **Specific** (names the real flaw, shows real fixes)? **Deadpan + succinct**? Does the **money beat read instantly**? Does it **close on the $5**?

Any "no" → fix the weakest, then ship.
