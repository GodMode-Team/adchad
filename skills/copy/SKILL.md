# Copy Pipeline

Use this for any paid-ads copy, landing-page copy, client-facing report, email, or marketing asset that leaves the team.

## Pipeline

1. Write in the team's voice first. Direct, specific, action-first, low fluff.
2. Run the quality gate. Stress-test with a ruthless competitor, cynical consumer, and distracted scroller.
3. Humanize. Remove AI patterns, corporate filler, generic hype, and over-polished phrasing.
4. Verify the platform fit. Ad copy should be simple, direct, compliant, and easy to understand fast.

## Paid ads rules

- Hook -> educate -> proof -> reason to act -> CTA.
- Use fifth-grade-simple language for cold traffic.
- Do not use fake urgency, unsupported clinical claims, or vague transformation language.
- Keep the ad, landing page, lead form, and offer aligned.
- For TRP public ads, avoid VSL, funnel, training, or watch-this-video mechanics. Sell diagnostic clarity and the Free 1:1 Coaching Call.
- For text-heavy graphics, use deterministic layout output like SVG, HTML/CSS, canvas, or Pillow so text stays readable.

## Quick QA

- Would the team actually say this out loud?
- Is the first line scroll-stopping?
- Is the offer obvious without sounding like junk?
- Would a skeptical patient/client know what happens next?
- Can 30 percent of the words be cut?
- Are there em dashes, corporate phrases, or AI tells that should be removed?

## Output format

Return the revised copy first, then a short note on what changed and any compliance/offer-alignment risks.
