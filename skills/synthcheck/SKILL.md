# SynthCheck

Use this when the user wants to test ad copy, offers, landing pages, lead forms, pricing, positioning, or campaign ideas before spending money.

## Non-negotiable method

Do not ask simulated people for a numeric rating directly. Ask for reactions in words first, then map those reactions to a score using Semantic Similarity Rating.

## Workflow

1. Define the decision and ship/kill threshold before testing.
2. Build 4-6 commercially relevant cohorts. Include a skeptic cohort and premium cohort.
3. Ground cohorts in real competitors, real prices, reviews, comments, sales-call objections, or CRM context when available.
4. Force heterogeneity: income, urgency, skepticism, purchase fatigue, life situation, and pain intensity.
5. Show each person one option when testing price. Rotate variants for copy, offers, names, or landing pages.
6. Collect natural-language reactions: what stops them, what they believe, what they remember, what they would click, and what they would ignore.
7. Score reactions against anchors. Report distributions, not only averages.
8. Apply an optimism haircut and recommend the smallest real-money smoke test.

## Paid ads outputs

- Winning hook or angle
- Objection map
- Believability risks
- CTA friction
- Lead-form friction
- Offer confusion
- Verbatim lines worth stealing
- Recommended real-money test

## Guardrails

SynthCheck is directional. It ranks options and surfaces objections, but it does not replace live spend data.
